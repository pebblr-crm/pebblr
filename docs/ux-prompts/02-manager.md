# Manager Persona

The manager oversees a subset of teams, monitors rep performance, and ensures visit targets are being met.

## What the Manager Sees

- **Teams:** The teams they manage, with all team members
- **All team activities:** Every activity for every rep in their teams — planned, completed, cancelled
- **All team targets:** Every target assigned to reps in their teams, including assignment distribution
- **Performance dashboards:** Coverage, frequency compliance, activity breakdowns per rep and per team
- **Alerts:** Business telemetry warnings (e.g., a rep falling behind on A-class visits, low coverage mid-quarter)
- **Target distribution:** How targets are divided across reps — balance and gaps

## Key Flows

### Monitoring Team Performance
1. View dashboard showing per-rep and per-team metrics:
   - **Coverage %:** What portion of assigned targets have been visited this period?
   - **Frequency compliance:** Per classification (A/B/C), are reps meeting required visit counts?
   - **Activity stats:** Breakdown by status (planned/completed/cancelled) and category (field/non-field)
2. Drill down into a specific rep's schedule and activity history
3. Identify reps who are behind and may need support or reassignment

### Target Oversight
- View all targets across managed teams
- See which targets are under-visited or unvisited
- Review target assignment balance across reps

### Activity Review
- View submitted activities and feedback from reps
- Spot patterns (e.g., high cancellation rates, clustering of visits)

### Alerts & Nudges
- Surface reps at risk of missing quarterly frequency targets
- Flag days with no planned activities or unusual patterns
- Recovery day balance visibility per rep

## UX Notes

- Managers need overview-first, detail-on-demand — don't bury them in raw data
- The primary question is: "Are my reps on track?" — answer it at a glance
- Comparison views (rep vs. rep, week vs. week) are valuable
- Managers don't typically create activities — they observe and intervene
